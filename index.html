Will Eagle

I'm an undergraduate Geology major, 2024 Udall Scholar, and Undergraduate Research Assistant at the Jackson School of Geosciences. My research interests include paleoclimate, karst, and water resources. Outside research, I work to build community through a peer mentorship program.

Texas Droughts — Dendrochronology and Ecohydrology

I’ve worked on using tree rings to reconstruct regional hydroclimate. Most recently I presented this work at GSA Connects 2025.

Cave Stable Isotope Modeling

Using numerical modeling to understand processes that impact cave drip water chemistry at Sitting Bull Falls.

Karst Hydrogeology

At the Bureau of Economic Geology, I am building streamflow monitoring networks and characterizing spring residence times using radon.
